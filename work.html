Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

1964-2015

Missile Man Of India

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (born October 15, 1931, Rameswaram, India—died July 27, 2015, Shillong) was an Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India’s missile and nuclear weapons programs. He was president of India from 2002 to 2007. Kalam earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology and in 1958 joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). In 1969 he moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation, where he was project director of the SLV-III, the first satellite launch vehicle that was both designed and produced in India. Rejoining DRDO in 1982, Kalam planned the program that produced a number of successful missiles, which helped earn him the nickname “Missile Man.” Among those successes was Agni, India’s first intermediate-range ballistic missile, which incorporated aspects of the SLV-III and was launched in 1989.

Biography

  • Birth Born on October 15, 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, into a poor Tamil Muslim family.
  • Education Attended Schwartz Higher Secondary School in Ramanathapuram and St. Joseph's College in Tiruchirappalli, where he graduated with a degree in Physics in 1954
  • Career Worked on India's space and missile development program, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India". He was also the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation from 1992 to 1999.
  • Presidency Elected as the 11th President of India in 2002 and served for five years. He was India's first bachelor president and was very popular with children
  • Awards Received the Bharat Ratna in 1997, the Padma Vibhushan in 1990, and the Padma Bhushan in 2011.
  • Death Died on July 27, 2015 at the age of 83 after falling and suffering a heart attack while giving a lecture at the Indian Institute in Shillong, Meghalaya
